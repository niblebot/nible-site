Slow Buffalo
A herd of buffalo can only move as fast as the slowest buffalo, and when the herd is hunted, it is the slowest and weakest ones at the back that are killed first. This natural selection is good for the herd as a whole, because the general speed and health of the whole keeps improving by the regular culling of the weakest members. In much the same way, the human brain can operate only as fast as the slowest brain cells. Excessive intake of alcohol, we all know, kills off brain cells, but naturally it attacks the slowest and weakest brain cells first. In this way, regular consumption of beer eliminates the weaker cells, constantly making the brain a faster and more efficient machine.
The result of this in-depth epidemiological study verifies and validates the causal link between all-weekend parties and work performance. It also explains why, after a few short years of leaving university and getting married, most people cannot keep up with the performance of the new graduates. Only those few that stick to the strict regimen of voracious alcoholic consumption and caning it profuselly can maintain the intellectual levels that they achieved during their university years. So, this is a call to arms. As our country is losing its technological edge we should not shudder in our homes. Get back into the bars! Quaff that pint! Skin up. Kick back. Live it. Fly. High. In the sky.

Contrary to beliefs there is an unknown element of systematic eradication in the kangaroo species. They have a natural instinct to destroy each others pouches, and as a result, one can see many a time when driving though what is now known as the kango wastelands, where the fighting is arranged, the remains of kangaroos with thier pouches ripped apart.

The force which a kangaroo can muster in order to complete the deadly act has been a secret for many years until now. Termed as the ROOROO force (Rotational Ordered Occulant Removal Of Objects), the power is incredibly stored in the gap between the upper eyelashes. When this force is required, the force is used by way of quantum lash swapping. Whereby the eyelashes swap with hairs on the kangaroos fingers almost instantaneously.

Scientists are now looking for a way to harness this force which when harnessed can power the likes of a whole city the size of London for 9 months and 9 minutes. We shall bring you up to date with any further developments from the Cartel Research Deparment.
