Harsh rain crashed against the outcrop as Horsforth huddled inside. Known as the Beast Man and feared from many angles, this loathsome creature was now rather damp. His three manes hung drenched at his feet as he surveyed the damage caused by the downpour. The mud flats that once aided his passage from Nurth to Bleem now were sodden and unpassable.

The air had a strange scent that night, a scent that made Horsforth very uneasy. He had heard tales of the great frost of ad121212, the year in which the new spell was created, but had attributed it always to folklore and legend.

As he sat now, becoming less coherent as each degree fell, his scepticism was rapidly diminishing. Could there really be truth to the fable?

As he sank slowly further into the corner of the cave, he became aware of another being close by. His conciousness level was now almost zero, and in this fluxate he could only discern the outline. And it was the most terrible vision. Never before had he seen such grotesque deformity, and never before had he been so terrified. As the being advanced, he became aware that he had lost control of his bodily functions. He felt ashamed. Then came the pain.

Then came the darkness.

When Opus had finished eating, he tossed the bone aside and gathered up his belongings. He had eaten well tonight, and would not need nourishing again for sometime. It was not often that a Chant was foolish enough to enter the mud flats during a frost. How long had the Chant been there, he pondered? Such an easy kill for one so old as he. As he bent to collect his robe, a blast tore open his chest with such force as to seperate his entire upper body.

Whirling round as his physical form reconverged, he saw a younger standing with a staff in his grasp. How could one so young summon such power from a simple sceptre?

Quick as a flash Opus teleported behind the younger and snapped his spine. Blue was not aware of any of this, for the sceptre was emitting a kind of neuro-anaesthetic that to Blue resembled a higher state. His death was instantaneous.

Opus had no desire to luncheon further, so he torched the corpse. The sceptre, on closer inspection, held greater interest for the warrior chief. Examining the head of the staff he was presented with a crest. This consisted of the number nine encircled by what looked like a serpent, although it could have been an anchove. Could it be? Could this really be the Sceptre of Noone? All of the markings were in place, but Opus was sure that the sceptre had been destroyed by the Queen of Multipacks. Only one way to find out for sure.

He held the sceptre aloft and, at the top extent of his telepathic capacity, he yelled "Dooooo Bahh Fneeeeet flail tea lung ka ba ra sa ta bahh dooooo!"

All at once he was engulfed in plasma. His transform became lateral, and the balance within the chubber loop was refracted. He screamed as all knowledge was bestowed upon him in an instant, and as Opus became temperal, the frost disappeared and the nine suns were once again visible through the dust clouds.

In Biradel the people panicked for they knew that the evil had returned. This time the terror would come from all around the town, and nowhere was safe. It was only a matter of time before Opus became hungry. After his ordeal, it would not be very long. Not very long at all.
